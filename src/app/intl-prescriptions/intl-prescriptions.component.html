To obtain a prescription from an out-of-country doctor while traveling, you'll generally need to have the prescription filled by a local doctor in your destination country, or potentially through a telehealth service. Pharmacies in most countries will only fill prescriptions issued by a doctor licensed in that country.

Here's a breakdown of the steps you can take:

  • Consult with your home country doctor:
    Obtain a copy of your prescription:
    Ensure you have a copy of your current prescription, including the generic name of the medication and a letter from your doctor explaining your medical condition and need for the medication.
    Request a digital copy:
    Ask your doctor to email you a digital copy of your prescription and the letter, in case you need to access them electronically.
  • Contact your travel insurance provider:
    Check coverage:
    Verify if your travel insurance covers prescription medications and if they have any specific procedures for obtaining them while abroad.
    Utilize their services:
    Many travel insurance companies offer assistance with finding doctors, pharmacies, and even arranging for medication refills through their network.
  • Find a local doctor (if needed):
    Consult your travel insurance:
    They can often recommend English-speaking doctors in your destination.
    Use online resources: Platforms like AllyzÂ® can help you locate healthcare providers.
    Consider telehealth:
    Some telehealth services, like Optum Now, allow you to consult with a U.S.-licensed doctor online and potentially get a prescription if appropriate.
  • Obtain a local prescription:
      Schedule an appointment:
    • Make an appointment with a local doctor and explain your situation, providing them with your prescription and doctor's letter.
    • Ensure proper documentation:
      Make sure the local doctor is aware of your medical condition, treatment plan, and the medication regimen.
    • Be prepared to pay:
      Understand that you will likely need to pay out-of-pocket for medications, as your U.S. health insurance card may not be accepted.
  • Refill your medication:
    • Check with local pharmacies
    • Present your local prescription to a pharmacy and inquire about the availability of your medication.


If you are staying in one location for an extended period, check if the pharmacy offers home delivery services.

Important Considerations:



Medication availability:

Some medications may not be available in your destination country or may have different brand names.

Controlled substances:

Be aware that controlled substances may have specific restrictions and may not be available for refills while traveling.

Legal restrictions:

Some countries have strict rules about importing medications, even if they are legally obtained elsewhere.

Emergency situations:

If you need emergency medication, contact your travel insurance provider or the nearest embassy or consulate for assistance.
