In Spain, the public healthcare system (Sistema Nacional de Salud - SNS) is virtually free at the point of use for residents and citizens. However, if you are not covered by the SNS and need medical care, you will be responsible for the full cost of treatment. Emergency room visits can cost around €200, while a doctor's appointment might be €40-50, according to Doctorsa.

Public Healthcare:

The SNS is the primary healthcare system, funded by taxes and providing free or low-cost care to those who qualify.

Who Qualifies:

Spanish citizens, residents with social security contributions, and those with reciprocal agreements (like EU citizens with an EHIC card) are generally eligible.

Non-Eligible Individuals:

Tourists, short-term visitors, or those not contributing to social security in Spain may not be covered by the SNS and will need to pay for services.

Emergency Care:

While emergency care is provided regardless of insurance status, ongoing treatment may require payment.

Private Healthcare:

If not eligible for public healthcare, private insurance or direct payment for services is necessary.

    Cost of Services:
  • Emergency Room: €200
  • Doctor's Visit: €40-50
  • Basic X-ray: €50-150
  • Blood Test: €80
  • MRI (various areas): €500-680

Prescriptions:

While prescription drugs may require a co-payment, the amount varies depending on income.

Private Healthcare Costs:

Private insurance costs vary, typically ranging from €50-200 per month, depending on age and coverage.

EU/EEA Citizens:

Those with an EHIC (European Health Insurance Card) can access necessary public healthcare during temporary stays, but it's not a substitute for full residency and insurance.
