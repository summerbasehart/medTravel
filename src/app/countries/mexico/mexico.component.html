In Mexico, healthcare costs for those without insurance can vary widely, from relatively affordable basic care to potentially very expensive emergency or specialized treatments.

Public Healthcare:

The Instituto de Salud para el Bienestar (INSABI) provides free or low-cost healthcare to uninsured Mexican citizens and residents.

Who Qualifies:

Mexican citizens and residents citizens, residents with social security contributions, and those with reciprocal agreements (like EU citizens with an EHIC card) are generally eligible.

Non-Eligible Individuals:

Tourists, short-term visitors, or those not contributing to social security in Mexiso may not be covered and will need to pay for services.

Emergency Care:

Emergency services are available to everyone, regardless of residency or insurance status, but costs may apply once the condition is stabilized.

Private Healthcare:

If not eligible for public healthcare, private insurance or direct payment for services is necessary.

    Cost of Services:
  • Emergency Room: $50-200
  • Doctor's Visit: $12-18
  • Basic X-ray: $150-200
  • Blood Test: $15-35
  • MRI (various areas): $110-826

Prescriptions:

While prescription drugs may require a co-payment, the amount varies depending on income.

Private Healthcare Costs:

Private insurance costs vary, typically ranging from $1000-3000 per year, depending on age and coverage.
