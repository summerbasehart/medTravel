In Canada, the public healthcare system provides universal coverage for citizens and permanent residents, meaning essential services are free at the point of service.

Public Healthcare:

For those covered by the public system, there are typically no direct costs (like co-pays or deductibles) for essential medical services received at hospitals and doctors' offices.

Who Qualifies:

Canada's public healthcare system, funded by taxes, provides universal coverage for citizens and permanent residents for medically necessary services.

Non-Eligible Individuals:

Tourists, short-term visitors, or those not contributing to social security in Canada may not be covered and will need to pay for services.

Emergency Care:

None residents will be billed for healthcare services at rates set by the province or territory.

Private Healthcare:

If not eligible for public healthcare, private insurance or direct payment for services is necessary.

    Cost of Services:
  • Emergency Room: $400-1100
  • Doctor's Visit: $100-900
  • Basic X-ray: $40-250
  • Blood Test: $30-100
  • MRI (various areas): $400-2500

Prescriptions:

While prescription drugs may require a co-payment, the amount varies depending on income.

Private Healthcare Costs:

Private insurance costs vary, typically ranging from $300-800 per month, depending on age and coverage.
